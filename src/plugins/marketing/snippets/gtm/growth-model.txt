# Growth model: AARRR metrics + growth loops

## AARRR "pirate metrics" (Dave McClure) - instrument the whole journey
- Acquisition - users arrive (from which channel?).
- Activation - first valuable experience (the "aha"); the #1 lever for most products.
- Retention - they come back (the FOUNDATION - without it the funnel leaks faster than you can fill it).
- Referral - they bring others.
- Revenue - they pay.
Find the leakiest step first; fix activation/retention before pouring money into acquisition.

## Growth loops > funnels (Brian Balfour / Reforge)
A funnel is linear (pour in the top, leak out the bottom). A LOOP reinvests the output back into the input:
  new users -> take an action producing an output (content, invites, data, revenue) -> that output drives NEW acquisition -> repeat.
Loop types: viral (users invite users), content (UGC -> indexable pages -> SEO -> users), paid (revenue funds ads that acquire revenue-generating users).
Design at least one loop; loops compound, funnels do not.

## North Star metric: one number capturing delivered customer value (nights booked, messages sent, docs created). Align the team to move it.
